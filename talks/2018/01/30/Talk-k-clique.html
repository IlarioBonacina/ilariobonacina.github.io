Clique is hard on average for regular resolution

Jan 30 , 2018 Dagstuhl Seminar 18051 – Proof Complexity – Slides

Deciding whether a graph G with n vertices has a -clique is one of the most basic computational problems on graphs. In this talk we show that certifying -clique-freeness of Erdos-Renyi random graphs is hard for regular resolution. More precisely we show that for regular resolution asymptotically almost surely requires length to establish that an Erdos-Renyi random graph (with appropriate edge density) does not contain a -clique. This asymptotically optimal result implies unconditional lower bunds on the running time of several state-of-the-art algorithms used in practice.
